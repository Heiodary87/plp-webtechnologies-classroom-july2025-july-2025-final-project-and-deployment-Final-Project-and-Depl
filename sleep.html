Sleep and Recovery

Your body repairs, your mind resets- sleep is your silent healer.

Sleep is an essential part of health-span-living. Research suggests that getting enough sleep allows your body to recharge, repair, and prepare for the next day. Sleep deprivation can affect your mood, cognitive function, and overall well-being.

Sleep Tracker

Sleep Tips

  • Stick to a regular sleep schedule, even on weekends.
  • Create a sleep-friendly environment with a cool, dark, and quiet room.
  • Limit caffeine and alcohol before bedtime.
  • Avoid screens before sleep to reduce blue light exposure.
  • Try relaxation techniques like deep breathing or meditation before bed.

Remember, sleep is a vital part of your health-span-living. Prioritize it for better well-being.
